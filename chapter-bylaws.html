THE SOCIETY OF HISPANIC PROFESSIONAL ENGINEERS: STONY BROOK CHAPTER

Article I: Name

        

The name of the organization shall be the Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers

Article II: Purpose

The Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers promotes the development of Hispanics in engineering, science and other technical professions to achieve educational excellence, economic opportunity and social equality.

The purpose of this student organization is to:

  • Promote professional awareness by means of social and intellectual interaction on campus and

across the nation.

  • Increase the number of Hispanic and minority students in the science, technology, engineering and mathematics fields (STEM) and other professional fields that lack high representation of these ethnic groups.
  • Help students stay in school by providing personal guidance and support.
  • Give students access to scholarships, internships and jobs around the nation.
  • Unite and help in the advancement of Hispanics and students based on needs of their issues and

concerns.

  • Promote cultural awareness in the community.

The Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers, as a registered organization at Stony Brook University is committed to promoting a safe and vibrant campus community. In receiving annual recognition through the Department of Student Engagement and Activities, no individual or group affiliated with the Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers will take any action or create a situation which recklessly or intentionally endangers mental or physical health or involves the forced consumption of liquor or drugs for the purpose of initiation into or affiliation with this organization. The leaders and members of the Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers also agree to abide by all aspects of the Stony Brook University Conduct of Student Responsibility, University policies and Federal, New York State and Local laws.

Article III: Membership

Section 1: Active/Voting Members

  1. Membership must be open to currently registered, matriculated undergraduate students of Stony Brook University who have paid the Undergraduate Student Activities fee and must subscribe to or be interested in the purpose of this organization. Membership in this organization shall be open to any undergraduate student in the University, regardless of what degree they wish to pursue. Only students who pay the student activity fee may pursue membership.
  2. Active membership status and privileges include but are not limited to fee waivers (or partial waivers), voting privileges (unless otherwise stated above), transportation to special events, access to resources and organizations off campus that the organization associates with.
  3. Active members have assured voting rights, however, their membership must be confirmed via a payment of membership dues (Section 3).
  4. No person shall be denied membership or office because of sexual orientation, nation of origin, race, sex, and physical disability, political or religious beliefs, and all other protected classes under federal and state laws.


Subsection A: Events and Activities

  1. Events include but are not limited to general body meetings, workshops, conferences, community service events, and any function sponsored by the organization.


Section 2: Non Voting Members

  1. The Faculty Advisor will be a non­-voting member.

Section 3: Membership dues

  1. USG funds may be used to pay for national dues of membership. Donations and fundraising may also be used to accommodate for national dues. Members are not required to pay out of pocket, and dues may not be mandated from the current or prospective membership.
  2. Dues payment of general body members is the responsibility of the executive board.


Section 4: Other Majors

  1. Any student of the University, regardless of what degree they wish to pursue, can join the organization as a ­voting member with active membership privileges. In order to achieve this status, the student must comply with the specified terms in Article III, Section 1.

Section 5: Loss of Status

  1. Members can and will temporarily lose their membership status, and all privileges associated with such status, if they are absent for at least 50% of the scheduled semester events or activities without a valid excuse. An Executive Member may temporarily lose their active status, and all privileges associated with such status, if they are absent for at least 75% of the scheduled semester events or activities. These members will be declared inactive and must appeal to the executive committee. Any further action shall be decided by the executive committee
  2. Banning members based on behavior


Article IV: Executive Board or Officers

Section 1: General Requirements

  1. All nominees for executive board positions must have a minimum, cumulative grade point average of 2.25 and if elected, must maintain a cumulative grade point average of  2.25 and must be a registered, matriculated student for the semester(s) during which they are to serve.
  2.  All officers shall serve for one academic year and must be re-elected at the end of their term, as long as they maintain a cumulative grade point average of 2.25 and remain as a registered, matriculated student each semester.

Section 2: Organization

  1. The members of the executive board shall be President, Vice President, Treasurer and Secretary.
  2. The total membership of the executive board shall not exceed members of the organization

Section 3: President

  1. Presides at all general meetings.
  2. Appoints all committee chairpersons.
  3. Prepares the annual report on the organization's activities.
  4. Acts as spokesman and overall official of organization.

Section 4: Vice President

  1. Performs all duties of the President in his/her absence, or at the request of the officer and shall assume any responsibilities assigned by the president.
  2. In the event that the position of the president becomes vacant, the Vice President shall assume office until the next extended board meeting.
  3. Services as the President's chief aide.
  4. Oversees all committee chairpersons and their duties.
  5. Responsible for all activities and events that are sponsored on campus.
  6. Acts as liaison between Stony Brook SHPE and relevant Stony Brook Departments such as the College of Engineering and Applied Sciences and the Career Center.
  7. Oversees the organization of activities and events that are sponsored off campus.
  8. Acts as liaison between Stony Brook SHPE and SHPE National, SHPE Foundation, the National Board of Directors, SHPE Jr. Chapter, and SHPE Professional Alumni.

Section 5: Treasurer

  1. Receives all funds of the organization and keeps detailed record of all monies allotted to the organization.
  2. Pays the bills and refunds as authorized by the executive committee, and submits all payment requests.
  3. Keeps an exact account of all receipts and expenditures and reports them all to the executive committee.
  4. Performs duties as necessary and proper in the fulfillment of his/her office, subject to the approval of the executive board.
  5. Assumes the position of Vice President in the event that the Vice President is unable to perform his/her duties (due to leave of absence, resignation, or assumption of Presidency). A new Treasurer will be elected in special election called by the President if a vacancy occurs in this office.
  6. Plans and writes all fundraising proposals and keep a record of them.

Section 6: Secretary

  1. Keeps a record of all meetings and organizational sessions.
  2. Keeps a record of all documents and maintains an attendance record.
  3. Distributes minutes from all meetings to the membership.
  4. Informs members of events, internships, and scholarships available.
  5. Keeps a record of all active members and alumni.
  6. Sends notice to members of National meetings and events.

Article V: Meetings

This organization shall hold one general membership meeting at least two times per month per semester or as determined by the executive committee.

Section 1: Quorum

Subsection A: General Meetings

  1. A quorum consisting of a majority of the total active members is necessary for the transaction of any business of all meetings.
  2. All meetings must have an agenda and give notice to members at least one school day prior.

Subsection B: Executive Meetings

  1. The quorum for executive committee shall be determined by the amount of members on the committee. If the organization has only officers and an advisor, then quorum shall be two voting executive members plus the President or Vice President. If the executive committee consists of officers, advisor, and chairpersons, then majority executive members plus the President or Vice President.

Article VI: Elections

Section 1: Selection of Officers

  1. Nominations for officers shall be made at the meeting prior to the elections. The members shall be notified of these nominations before elections are held. No person shall seek the position of President, Co-Chair, Vice President, Treasurer, or Secretary unless he/she has been a member of this organization for two semesters prior to the semester when he/she would assume office.
  2. An election committee shall be elected by the general membership in March of each academic year. It shall consist of five (5) members, none of whom may be considered for elected office. If a vacancy occurs on the committee, a special election shall be held to fill the vacant seat. This committee shall elect its own chairperson and recommend the time, manner, and pace of election pursuant to the articles contained in this constitution.
  3. The officers positions will be voted on individually, starting with the President, followed by the Vice President, Secretary, Treasurer. Any member running for a position that is not elected may run for another position that has not been voted on during elections without being re-­nominated, as stated in Article IV, Subsection D.
  4. All officers must be elected by majority vote. Elections must be held no later than May 1st. New officers will assume their positions on June 1st.

Section 2: Reopening Nominations

  1. Nominations will remain open until the day of elections. Nominations can be reopened if there is a majority vote among the membership.

Section 3: Officer’s Tenure Length

  1. Elected officers shall take office one month after elections or when the position is available, whichever is shorter of the two. An officer’s term will be for two consecutive semesters after taking office. Officers that wish to rerun for their current or past position must go through the election process again. An officer can end the term at any time by handing in a formal letter of resignation to the executive committee.


Section 4: Reelection

  1. There shall be no limit to the number of terms an officer can run for their position while eligible through Article III, Section 1. However, this re-­election process must pursue the rules stated Article IV, Section 1A.


Section 5: Vacancies and Special Elections

  1. In the event of vacancy of any executive officer position, a special election must be held. Special elections will consist of electing new officers by a majority vote. Elections must be held within a one month period from the time of a vacancy.
  2. Nominations shall be accepted from the floor at a meeting prior to the election.
  3. Election shall be by majority vote of the active voting members present whose names appear on the membership roster.


Subsection A: Vacancy of President and Other Positions

  1. Impeachment of President or Co-Chair, the Vice President assumes the Presidency of Co-Chair position and a special election is held for Vice President within a one month period.
  2. Impeachment of other officers, a special election will be held within a one month period.

Section 6: Committees

  1. All active members may participate in a committee and shall not be excluded from joining one. The committees will carry out the purpose and aims of the organization. Responsibilities shall be split in a committee as the chairpersons sees fit. Committee members report to their respective chairperson.

Subsection A: Committee Chairperson

  1. The President shall appoint the committee chairperson, with the approval of the primary executive board (i.e. officers). A member must either, petition or, be asked by the President to be considered for a chair position. The President may create and appoint as many committees as required of the organization. These chairpersons will report to the Vice President of any progress or lack of progress. Chairpersons will make up the extended executive board.


Subsection B: Removal of Committee Chairperson

  1. If any formal complaint is brought forth about a chairperson, the removal of this individual will follow the same procedures as impeachment (Article VII)

Article VII: Impeachment

Any officers of the club may be brought up on impeachment charges if he/she is acting detrimental to the organization or he/she has significantly disregarded the major responsibilities of his/her office.


Section 1: Petition for Impeachment by Executive Board

  1. If a list of charges signed by three-quarters (3/4) of the officers is submitted to the President, he/she must call for an impeachment proceeding. If the President is being brought up on charges, the petition shall be submitted to the Vice President. Upon receipt of the petition, the President or Co-Chair shall suspend the officer and prevent him/her from exercising all powers and voting rights pending the outcome of the impeachment proceedings.


Section 2: Petition for Impeachment by Active members

  1. If a list of charges signed by two-thirds (2/3) of the active members is submitted to the President or Co-Chair, he/she must call for impeachment proceedings.If the President is being brought up on charges, the petition shall be submitted to the Vice President. Upon receipt of the petition, the President or Co-Chair shall suspend the officer and prevent him/her from exercising all powers and voting rights pending the outcome of the impeachment proceedings.

Section 3: Voting of Impeachment and Officer Removal

  1. After the proper impeachment proceedings, the officer(s) so charged may be impeached by a three-quarter (3/4) vote of group members. If impeachment passes, the officer shall be removed from his/her position.


Section 4: Reasons of Impeachment

  1. Officers may be impeached based on but not limited to the loss of active membership status, being irresponsible of their duties stated in Article IV and lacking any responsibility to the membership or organization.


Article VIII: Amendments

  1. Amendments to this constitution may be introduced from the floor and must be submitted to the Secretary in writing at a general body membership meeting. This constitution may be amended by a 2/3 vote of the members present. Notice of proposed amendments to the constitution shall be provided to the members with the Notice of Meeting.
  2. The entire voting membership shall be notified in writing at least five (5) school days prior to vote on a proposed amendment.
  3. A two-thirds (2/3) vote of the active voting membership is necessary for the passage of an amendment.

Article IX: Dissolution

The Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers  will dissolve if ⅔ majority of quorum voters vote to do so. The club could also be dissolved without the club’s consent, such as the University’s decision.  In the event of the dissolution of the club, all USG accrued funds and assets shall revert to the Undergraduate Student Government. The club’s personal money will be donated to the Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers National Chapter. All property belonging to the organization will be sold, with profits being donated to the Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers National Chapter as well.

The Society of Hipanic Professional Engineers at Stony Brook must adhere to the articles in the constitution in order to hold a strong chapter image to not only the national organization, but to the community of Stony Brook.
